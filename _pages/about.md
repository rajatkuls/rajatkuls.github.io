I’m an **ML generalist with a specialization in audio and speech**, currently an Applied Scientist on the AGI Foundations team at Amazon. I’m a core contributor to the Nova 2 family of multimodal LLMs, where I focus on **multimodal post-training and evaluation** for real-time production use.

My work is centered on making AI usable in the real world: **dependable** in production, **aligned** with user expectations, and **responsive** in real-time. I’ve led spoken interaction quality work, shipped product-aligned model improvements, and built evaluation, benchmarking, and release-gating pipelines that enable faster iteration without regressions.

Previously, I was a **Founding Engineer** at Telling.ai, where I led development of speech-first AI systems for longitudinal respiratory health monitoring using voice and breath sounds, and helped take research prototypes into clinical-grade production systems.

I have over 8 years of experience in AI research and engineering. I hold a research master’s degree in Language Technologies from Carnegie Mellon University and a bachelor’s degree from IIT Guwahati.

Outside of work, I enjoy obsessively optimizing my coffee—and then applying the same mindset to rapidly prototyping ideas. A recent project, [LLM Powered After Visit Summary](https://eyekapoor.com), was selected for a panel discussion at the American Academy of Ophthalmology.
